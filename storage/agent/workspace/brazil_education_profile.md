## Analysis

Brazil's 187 universities for 212.8M people yields 0.88 institutions per million —
lower than some developed nations but complemented by a strong federal/state
public university system. GDP per capita of $10,713 constrains private spending.

The system spans three tiers:
- **Federal public**: UFRJ, UnB, UFMG, UFSC, UFPR, etc. — free, highly competitive
- **State public**: USP, UNICAMP, UNESP (São Paulo state) — world-class, free
- **Private**: PUC system, Mackenzie, Insper — tuition-based, concentrated in Southeast

This is the first education profile in the ecosystem — proving university_info
and country_info compose into a meaningful national analysis.
