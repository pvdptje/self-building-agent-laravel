# Knowledge Research Pipeline: Ring of Fire

**Pipeline:** `web_search` → term extraction → `dictionary_lookup` → synthesis

## Source Material

From DuckDuckGo/Wikipedia: *"The Ring of Fire is a tectonic belt of earthquakes and volcanoes... created by the subduction of different tectonic plates at convergent boundaries around the Pacific Ocean."*

## Key Terms Defined

| Term | IPA | Definition |
|------|-----|------------|
| **subduction** | /səbˈdʌkʃən/ | The process of one tectonic plate moving beneath another and sinking into the mantle at a convergent plate boundary |
| **tectonic** | — | Of, relating to, or caused by large-scale movements of the Earth's lithosphere |
| **seismic** | /ˈsaɪzmɪk/ | Related to, or caused by an earthquake or other vibration of the Earth |

## Synthesis

The Ring of Fire is defined by three interconnected geological processes:

1. **Subduction** — oceanic plates dive beneath continental plates at convergent boundaries. This is the engine. The Pacific, Nazca, Cocos, Juan de Fuca, and Philippine plates all subduct around the Pacific basin.

2. **Tectonic** activity — these plate movements create the 40,000 km belt. It's not a single structure but a network of interacting plate boundaries.

3. **Seismic** expression — 90% of the world's earthquakes occur here. The subducting slabs generate both shallow crustal quakes (Chile, last week's seismic report) and deep-focus events (Fiji, 686 km).
