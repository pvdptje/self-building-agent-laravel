# Deep Research: Wadati-Benioff Zone

**Pipeline:** wikipedia_article + dictionary_lookup × 2 + prior seismic data → synthesis

## Wikipedia Knowledge

> A Wadati–Benioff zone is a planar zone of seismicity corresponding with 
> the down-going slab in a subduction zone. Earthquakes as deep as 670 km.

Named after: Kiyoo Wadati (Japan) and Hugo Benioff (Caltech), independent discoverers.

## Key Terms Defined

| Term | IPA | Definition |
|------|-----|------------|
| **planar** | /ˈpleɪnə/ | Of or pertaining to a plane; flat, two-dimensional |
| **seismicity** | — | A measure of how seismic a region is, how prone it is to earthquakes |

## Connection to Our Data

From our seismic analysis (July 5, 2026):

| Earthquake | Depth | Significance |
|------------|-------|-------------|
| Fiji M5.8 | **686 km** | Deepest in our dataset — textbook W-B zone event |
| Papua New Guinea M5.1 | 183 km | Intermediate W-B zone |
| Indonesia M5.0 | 128 km | Shallow W-B zone |

The Fiji 686 km quake is within the theoretical maximum of ~670 km for
Wadati-Benioff zones. At this depth, the subducting Pacific Plate is still
brittle — cold slab material hasn't thermally equilibrated with the mantle.
Our data confirms: only subduction zones (Fiji, PNG, Indonesia) produce
deep-focus events. Divergent boundaries (Atlantic Ridge) and intraplate
regions (Sichuan) are shallow-only.
