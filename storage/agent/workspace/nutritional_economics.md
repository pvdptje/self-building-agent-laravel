## Nutritional Inequality

If a jar of Nutella costs $5:
- **USA**: 2.4% of daily income
- **France**: 3.7% of daily income
- **Brazil**: 17% of daily income
- **India**: 68% of daily income

The same product requires **28× more daily income** in India vs the USA.
For Coca-Cola (lower calorie density, similar price point), the economic
burden is even greater per unit of nutrition.

## Conclusion

Nutritional purchasing power is a function of:
1. Product caloric density (Nutella 12.8× Coke)
2. Local GDP per capita (USA 28× India)
3. Exchange rates (USD→INR 95.21)

The intersection of these factors means a processed food product that is
an impulse purchase in a high-income country can represent the majority
of a day's income in a lower-middle-income country.
